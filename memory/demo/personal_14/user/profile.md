Caleb Martin Hebert is a floodplain outreach specialist at the Harris County
Flood Control District, living in Humble, Texas. He holds a degree in Geography
and describes himself as a patient explainer — quietly stubborn, conflict-averse
but reliable, and a storm-season worrier who processes stress through visible
artifacts and structured systems.

Over several years he built and defended a concentrated corridor follow-through
program in eastern Harris County, moving households from workshops through
application, scope agreement, and construction starts. His method — block-level
scoreboards, 30/60/90-day milestone check-ins, and a named contact per household
— became the program standard by late 2025.

He's married to Lena, an occupational therapy assistant in Aldine ISD, and helps
coordinate care for his elderly mother Dorothy nearby. He has a black Lab named
Roux, follows the Houston Astros (paper scorekeeping is his mental reset), listens
to Texas country and Gulf Coast classic rock, and reads nonfiction about Gulf
Coast history, mapping, and storms. He prefers practical, grounded help that
respects real constraints and plain-language translations of complex systems.
